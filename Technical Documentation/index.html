Div

Divs are essential for organization and for ease of use in CSS. Though there are some new elements in HTML5 that are essentailly specific use case divs; Divs are still heavily used. For example, if you wanted a section with a picture next to a paragraph you could put in all in a div, then puth both the image and the paragraph in their own respective divs for ease of use in css. an example of what I'm taling about-

<div>
 <div>
  <img>
 </div>
 <div>
  <p></p>
 </div>
</div>

Some specific divs were made to help support organization and screen readers. There names are pretty self explanitory, here are a few-

  • <main> - for the main content of the page
  • <section> - for subject sections of the page
  • <header> - for the heading of a page
  • <nav> - for page/site navigation menus
  • <article> - for articles of information that can stand alone
